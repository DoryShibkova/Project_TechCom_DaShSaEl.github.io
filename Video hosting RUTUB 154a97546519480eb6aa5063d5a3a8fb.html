Rutube is a video-sharing platform designed to deliver a seamless video experience for creators and viewers alike. The platform emphasizes community-driven interactions, personalized content discovery, and advanced content management.

Key Features

  1. Global Library of Movies and Series: Access to an extensive collection of films and TV shows across multiple genres and languages, catering to a diverse audience.
  1. Content Hosting and Streaming: Upload and watch high-quality videos.
  1. Personalized Recommendations: algorithms suggest content tailored to individual preferences.
  1. Community Features: Comments, likes, shares, and subscriptions for audience engagement.
  1. Content Moderation: Tools for reporting inappropriate content and managing a safe platform.
  1. Monetization Options: Ad placements, creator subscriptions, and direct fan support.
  1. Collaboration with Yappy: Show short videos from another service.

Prototyping

Intended Audience: UI/UX designers, product managers, testers

Purpose: Create interactive prototypes to simulate user journeys for content creators, viewers, and advertisers, and validate usability.

Collaboration: UI/UX designers, product managers, testers, user research teams

Technologies: Figma, InVision, UsabilityHub

Metric: Achieve a usability score of 4.5/5 in post-test surveys.

  • Plan of Communication
    • Create interactive prototypes simulating user journeys for content creators, viewers, and advertisers.
    • Conduct tests with a selected user group to evaluate ease of use and intuitiveness.
    • Use surveys and interviews to gather user feedback on prototypes.
    • Incorporate user feedback into updated prototypes.
  • Examples
    • Content Creator Journey Scenario

      Goal: Upload a video, set monetization preferences, and publish content.

      Steps in Prototype:

      1. Dashboard Access:
        • User logs into their dashboard.
        • Prototype shows the navigation menu with "Upload Video" prominently placed.
      1. Video Upload Workflow:
        • User selects a video file using drag-and-drop functionality or the "Browse" button.
        • Upload progress bar displays real-time status.
      1. Monetization Setup:
        • Prototype presents a form where the user chooses monetization options (ads, fan support, subscriptions).
        • Interactive tooltips explain each option.
      1. Content Review and Publish:
        • User previews the video and associated metadata (title, description, tags).
        • The "Publish" button becomes active, completing the workflow.

      Feedback Testing:

      • Conduct usability tests with creators.
      • Evaluate their ability to complete the journey without confusion.
      • Collect feedback on clarity and intuitiveness of the monetization setup.

      Target Metric: Achieve 4.5/5 usability score for the video upload process.

    • Viewer Journey Scenario

      Goal: Watch a recommended video and interact with community features.

      Steps in Prototype:

      1. Homepage Recommendations:
        • Viewer lands on the homepage, seeing personalized video thumbnails.
        • Interactive elements simulate hover effects and quick descriptions.
      1. Video Playback:
        • Clicking a thumbnail starts the video.
        • Controls for play, pause, and fullscreen mode are fully interactive.
      1. Community Engagement:
        • Prototype includes "Like," "Comment," and "Share" buttons.
        • Users can simulate adding a comment or sharing the video via social media links.

      Feedback Testing:

      • Observe testers navigating through recommendations and interacting with the video player.
      • Collect feedback on the layout and ease of finding community features.

      Target Metric: Achieve 4.5/5 usability score for viewer interaction flow.

    • Advertiser Journey Scenario

      Goal: Create and manage an ad campaign.

      Steps in Prototype:

      1. Dashboard Login:
        • Advertiser logs in and sees an overview of campaign performance metrics.
      1. New Campaign Creation:
        • Prototype guides the user through selecting an audience (e.g., age, location).
        • Budget and ad duration settings are interactive sliders.
      1. Ad Preview:
        • User uploads ad content and previews how it will appear during playback.
      1. Submit Campaign:
        • Prototype includes a "Submit" button that confirms the campaign setup.

      Feedback Testing:

      • Test with advertisers to evaluate the ease of campaign creation and preview functionality.
      • Collect suggestions for additional features.

      Target Metric: Achieve 4.5/5 usability score for ad campaign setup.

    Tools Used:

    • Figma: For building interactive prototypes.
    • UsabilityHub: For collecting feedback and scoring usability tests.
    • Google Forms: For additional feedback surveys

Issue Tracking

Intended Audience: Support teams, developers, project managers

Purpose: Track and resolve reported issues efficiently by documenting and prioritizing them based on severity.

Collaboration: Support teams, developers, project managers

Technologies: Jira, Confluence

Metric: Resolve 90% of critical issues within 24 hours and 80% of high-priority issues within 48 hours.

  • Plan of Communication
    • Workflow in Jira with the statuses:
      • Reported → In Progress → Resolved → Verified → Closed.
    • Reporting Issues
      • Testers and Support Teams:
        • Log detailed bug reports in Jira, including:
          • Title and description of the issue.
          • Steps to reproduce.
          • Severity and priority level.
          • Attachments (screenshots, logs).
        • Notify developers through Slack or Teams for critical issues.
      • End-Users (via Support Channels):
        • Support team triages issues reported by users through email or Google Forms.
        • Escalate validated issues to Jira for resolution.
    • Assigning Issues
      • Project manager assigns issues to the relevant developer based on expertise.
      • Developers acknowledge assigned tasks and update Jira status to "In Progress."
    • Resolution Workflow
      • Developers:
        • Fix the issue and update Jira with the solution.
        • Mark the issue as "Resolved."
      • Testers:
        • Verify the fix by replicating the issue scenario.
        • Update Jira status to "Verified" or reassign if unresolved.
  • Example
    • Workflow:
      • A bug is reported (e.g., video playback issues on specific browsers).
      • Developers reproduce and fix the bug.
      • Testers verify the fix before it is pushed to production.
    • Metrics:
      • Resolve 90% of critical issues within 24 hours and 80% of high-priority issues within 48 hours
